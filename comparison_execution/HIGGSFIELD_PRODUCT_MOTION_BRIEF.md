# Neeli PDP — Higgsfield product-motion brief

## 1. Recommendation

Use **one primary product-material clip** and at most **one supporting process or ritual clip**.

The Seed reference demonstrates that product motion can create material presence and maintain attention. Neeli should borrow that function without matching Seed's volume of video, production spectacle or premium-science tone.

Primary recommendation:

- Produce the **Violet-drop material loop**.
- Place it in the texture/product-understanding portion of the gallery or immediately below the hero.
- Keep the hero LCP as a static real-product image.
- Test a second hero treatment only after the material clip is approved.

Expected score effect:

- Image and visual merchandising: +0.2 to +0.4 weighted points.
- Persuasion and belief-building: +0.1 to +0.3.
- Motion and system feedback: +0.1 to +0.2.
- Possible net contribution: approximately **+0.4 to +0.8 points**.

This gain disappears if the clip distorts the label, misrepresents the product or damages Core Web Vitals.

## 2. Ranked concepts

### Concept A — Violet-drop material loop

**Priority:** P0  
**Source:** `04-oil-texture.jpg`  
**Purpose:** Communicate the oil's real colour, viscosity and sensory weight.

Visual sequence:

1. Locked macro view of the spoon and brass vessel.
2. The violet-brown droplet slowly elongates.
3. One drop falls into the vessel.
4. A restrained concentric ripple catches the warm light.
5. The camera makes a very slow push toward the oil surface.

Motion vocabulary:

- Orchestration.
- Slow camera translate/push.
- Follow-through in the droplet.
- Ease-in-out for the camera.
- A single loop or one-time playback, not perpetual ambient motion.

Production:

- Use the existing frame as the start image.
- Generate a 4–5 second image-to-video clip with Seedance 2.0.
- Produce a landscape and mobile crop only after the master motion is approved.
- Keep the spoon, oil colour and brass material tack sharp.

Placement:

- Gallery frame 3 or 4.
- Texture/fit section.
- Optional inline replay control.

Why this is the strongest option:

- It shows a decision-relevant product property.
- It contains no label typography for the model to distort.
- It does not imply hair-growth results.
- It can feel hyper-real without making the product look unaffordably luxurious.

### Concept B — Product-presence light reveal

**Priority:** P1  
**Sources:** official product cutout plus `01-hero-product.jpg`  
**Purpose:** Give the bottle Seed-like material presence while preserving label accuracy.

Visual sequence:

1. The real bottle remains stable and readable.
2. Warm botanical shadows move slowly across the background.
3. A narrow highlight travels across the glass and cap.
4. The camera performs a restrained 4–8px push.
5. The background settles into the static poster composition.

Motion vocabulary:

- Reveal.
- Continuity transition.
- Masked light sweep.
- Subtle translate.
- Orchestration.

Production:

- Create or refine the environment plate through the Higgsfield product-photoshoot `hero_banner` or `conceptual_product` mode.
- Animate only the environment and lighting with Seedance 2.0.
- Composite the official bottle/label above the generated motion.
- Do not ask the video model to rotate or regenerate the bottle.

Placement:

- Optional desktop hero enhancement after the static hero has loaded.
- Mobile remains poster-first, with a play control or a once-only post-LCP reveal.

### Concept C — Triple-milk convergence

**Priority:** P1  
**Source:** `05-triple-milk.jpg`  
**Purpose:** Make the distinctive formula structure memorable.

Visual sequence:

1. Three overhead vessels show small, natural surface movement.
2. Thin light paths connect the vessels toward a central formula marker.
3. The paths resolve into one line that joins the existing two-path recovery graphic.
4. HTML labels identify each approved component and its role.

Motion vocabulary:

- Line drawing.
- Orchestration.
- Stagger.
- Continuity transition.

Production:

- Keep the fluid footage natural and restrained.
- Add connecting lines and labels in HTML/SVG, not inside the generated video.
- Treat the convergence as an editorial metaphor, not a chemical or efficacy demonstration.

Placement:

- Formula section only.
- Never in the hero.

### Concept D — Three-drop ritual

**Priority:** P2  
**Sources:** `06-contemporary-ritual.jpg` plus an approved close-up product-with-person plate  
**Purpose:** Show quantity, application and massage behavior.

Visual sequence:

1. Fingers part the hair.
2. A small, explicit quantity is placed at the scalp.
3. Fingertips begin a gentle massage.
4. The sequence ends before implying a cosmetic transformation.

Motion vocabulary:

- Orchestration.
- Direct manipulation.
- Slow follow-through in hair movement.
- Crossfade to the static routine steps.

Production:

- Generate or refine the start frame through `closeup_product_with_person`.
- Animate with Seedance 2.0.
- Use a 4–6 second user-initiated clip.
- Closely review fingers, scalp contact, oil quantity and hair physics.

Placement:

- “How to use” section.
- Do not autoplay.

### Concept E — Slow-cooked craft

**Priority:** P2, conditional  
**Source:** `supporting-slow-cooking.jpg`  
**Purpose:** Support the process or heritage story.

Visual sequence:

1. Steam rises from the vessel.
2. A hand performs one slow stir.
3. Light catches the violet mixture.
4. The scene settles into its poster frame.

Use only if:

- The represented process is verified as materially accurate.
- The vessel, method and setting are not presented as documentary evidence unless they are real.

Placement:

- Lower heritage/process section.
- Never near testing or certification language.

## 3. Concepts to reject

- AI-generated 360-degree bottle rotation.
- A morph from ingredients into the finished bottle.
- Botanicals flying into the bottle.
- Hair becoming thicker, darker or longer.
- Before/after wipes using generated people.
- Infinite oil splashes, levitating ingredients or luxury-jewellery camera moves.
- A continuously autoplaying hero video.
- Text, claims or label details generated inside the clip.

These treatments either create proof risk, distort packaging, increase cognitive load or position the product above its actual price tier.

## 4. Higgsfield production workflow

### Step 1 — Lock source truth

- Use the official product cutout for every frame in which the label is readable.
- Confirm oil colour and viscosity.
- Confirm the three-milk components and approved formula language.
- Confirm application amount and slow-cooking/process accuracy.

### Step 2 — Build still plates

- Reuse the existing oil-texture and ritual candidates where suitable.
- Use Higgsfield product-photoshoot modes for missing plates:
  - `hero_banner` for the responsive hero environment.
  - `conceptual_product` for a controlled product/material composition.
  - `closeup_product_with_person` for the application frame.
- Generate stills at 2K.
- Approve still fidelity before introducing motion.

### Step 3 — Animate

- Use Seedance 2.0 image-to-video.
- Anchor each clip with a start image; use an end image when the final composition must be exact.
- Keep each clip between 4 and 6 seconds.
- Describe motion, camera and lighting rather than redescribing the source frame.
- Produce one master version before adapting aspect ratios.

### Step 4 — Protect the product

- Composite the official bottle and label over generated background motion.
- Keep product scale and perspective fixed unless a real multi-angle source exists.
- Put all explanatory copy, labels and claims in HTML/SVG.

### Step 5 — Review

Reject any frame containing:

- Label drift or invented typography.
- Bottle/cap geometry changes.
- Incorrect oil colour or watery viscosity.
- Implausible hands, scalp contact or hair movement.
- Botanically incorrect ingredients.
- Simulated results, testing or production proof.

### Step 6 — Encode and integrate

- Export WebM/AV1 where supported with an H.264 fallback.
- Provide a responsive poster for every clip.
- Target approximately 1.5MB or less for mobile and 2.5MB or less for desktop.
- Use `muted`, `playsinline` and `preload="metadata"`.
- Defer video loading until after the hero image/LCP.
- Allow only one clip to play at a time.
- Pause off-screen media.
- Under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, show the poster and play control only.

### Step 7 — Measure

Compare against a static control:

- Product-gallery engagement.
- Texture/routine section reach.
- Video play and completion.
- Add-to-cart rate.
- Mobile LCP and INP.
- Exit rate after the texture and routine modules.

Keep the animation only if it improves product understanding or conversion without a meaningful performance regression.

## 5. Recommended first production test

Produce three variants of **Concept A — Violet-drop material loop**:

1. Pure hyper-real macro.
2. Slightly more stylized warm editorial lighting.
3. A restrained botanical-shadow version.

Choose the version that most accurately communicates the actual oil before generating additional clips.
