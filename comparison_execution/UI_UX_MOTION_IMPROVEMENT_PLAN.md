# Neeli PDP — UI/UX and motion improvement plan

## 1. Direction

Build the final prototype as an **editorial recovery path**:

- Prototype B supplies the responsive shell, purchase system, image delivery and interaction base.
- Prototype A supplies the cold-skeptic persuasion arc, timeline, formula depth, comparison and objection coverage.
- The existing dual-track recovery line remains the single memorable device: reduce fall + support growth → one recovery path.

The design becomes more distinctive through typography, image scale, asymmetry and information rhythm. It does not become louder through glass, gradients, excessive cards, perpetual motion or decorative effects.

Target quality:

- Current B baseline: **63.5/100**.
- Minimum release gate: **85+/100**, with no criterion below 3.5/5.
- Seed-informed design target: **90–93/100**, with no criterion below 4.0/5.
- Validation stretch: **94–95/100** after real reviews, approved substantiation, production CWV and observed shopper testing.
- No unresolved P0/P1 findings.

## 3. UI concept: editorial recovery path

### Signature composition

The opening experience should communicate:

1. What the product is.
2. The two-part recovery promise.
3. The real pack and texture.
4. Price, size, delivery and action.
5. One approved reason to continue researching.

The recovery line begins as a subtle two-track marker near the promise, becomes the anchor-nav active indicator, and resolves into one line at the expected-results sequence. It must never become decorative wallpaper.

### Tone

- Modern natural efficacy.
- Evidence-seeking, calm and assured.
- Editorial rather than clinical dashboard.
- Ayurvedic provenance without nostalgic motifs.
- Premium through proportion, photography and precision.

### Risk budget

Push:

- Editorial 7/5 or 60/40 desktop composition.
- Large accurate product imagery.
- Strong display/body contrast.
- Alternating open and dark editorial sections.
- Purposeful full-bleed imagery at major belief moments.

Do not push:

- Purchase conventions.
- Label legibility.
- Accessibility.
- Claim language.
- Sticky-layer complexity.
- Motion frequency.

## 4. Visual hierarchy improvements

### 4.1 Hero

Replace B's current visually strong but commerce-light desktop opening with:

- Left or right editorial media field.
- Product decision panel containing name, approved promise, price, MRP, discount, size, delivery/COD, quantity and Add to cart.
- One factual research prompt: “See what to expect” or “See the complete formula.”
- No rating, review count, named quote or buyer count.
- One dominant rust purchase action; all other links use green/gold/ink.

Mobile order:

1. Product category/eyebrow.
2. Product name and two-line promise.
3. Primary product image.
4. One fit cue.
5. Price, size, delivery.
6. Purchase dock.
7. Gallery story selector.

The first two mobile viewports must contain recognition, relevance, one reason to believe and an actionable price.

### 4.2 Type hierarchy

Create a smaller, deliberate type system:

- Display: product name and major section statements only.
- UI/body: product facts, actions and explanations.
- Eyebrow: short all-caps labels only.
- Numeric: price, quantity and timeline with tabular figures where values change.

Increase contrast between levels rather than making every heading large. Collapse the current number of body sizes and opacity steps.

### 4.3 Spatial rhythm

Use a restrained spacing scale and three section densities:

- **Decision density:** hero, purchase and product facts.
- **Reading density:** formula, FAQ and comparison.
- **Gallery density:** timeline, ritual and approved heritage.

Group with whitespace, rules and surface changes. Use cards only for controls, stateful disclosures or information requiring containment.

### 4.4 Color and materiality

- Ksheera/cream remains the dominant field.
- Tulsi green remains the structural ink/dark surface.
- Rust remains purchase-only.
- Gold remains a small orientation or provenance accent.
- Remove opacity-based text that falls below contrast floors.
- Use subtle tinted shadows only where elevation communicates a functional layer.

## 5. Shopper-decision UX improvements

### Scan

- Eliminate unsupported proof and competing microclaims from the hero.
- Make the real pack, promise, price and action the first scan path.
- Keep the main gallery selector visually secondary to the primary frame.

### Shortlist

Use this sequence:

1. Honest expected-results timeline.
2. How the formula supports the two recovery goals.
3. Texture, routine and commitment.
4. Balanced treatment/category comparison.
5. Review-platform scaffold.
6. Detailed FAQ and heritage.

Add a short “Is this right for me?” summary that links to texture, routine, ingredients and cautions without becoming a quiz.

### Deselect

Create one concise product-fit module with:

- Texture and scent.
- Application amount.
- Frequency and wash time.
- Coconut-oil solidification.
- External-use note.
- Full ingredient access.
- Approved cautions and treatment-combination guidance.

This information must be visible before the final closing CTA.

### Decide

Use a single purchase state shared by hero, dock and drawer:

- Ready.
- Pressed.
- Adding.
- Added.
- View cart.
- Error/retry.
- Disabled.
- Out of stock.

Price, quantity and selected variant must remain synchronized everywhere.

## 6. Image-system improvements

Use B's responsive delivery and interaction model with six decision jobs:

| Sequence | Job | Visual requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Recognize | Accurate real product pack, label dominant |
| 2 | Relate | Scalp/application context, realistic touch |
| 3 | Qualify | Honest violet-brown oil texture and amount |
| 4 | Understand | Formula/ingredient system, not decorative bowls alone |
| 5 | Verify | Readable back label/ingredient panel with zoom |
| 6 | Set expectations | Ritual or neutral expectation context, never simulated results proof |

Rules:

- One image per shopper question.
- Remove repeated gallery/lower-section imagery.
- Use mobile-specific crops, not only smaller files.
- Keep subject, bottle label and hand/scalp action inside safe crop zones.
- Essential claims remain HTML text.
- Heritage and label imagery use approved real sources.
- No generated customer, certification or documentary proof.

## 7. Responsive design plan

### 320–699px

- One column.
- Compact header and horizontally scrollable anchor row.
- Purchase dock approximately 72–78px plus safe-area inset.
- Document reserves dock height.
- No simultaneous sticky gallery.
- Gallery uses swipe plus explicit 44px buttons.
- Ingredients/comparison/FAQ use progressive disclosure.
- Active anchor auto-centers and remains keyboard accessible.

### 700–899px

- Replace the current long stacked tablet hero with a compact two-zone composition.
- Primary image and decision summary appear side by side when each can retain at least a comfortable reading width.
- Move thumbnails/story navigation below the hero image if the split becomes cramped.
- Keep the purchase dock for touch use.
- Use two-column lower modules selectively.

### 900–1439px

- Editorial 7/5 or 60/40 hero split.
- Price and action remain visible in the decision panel.
- Sticky anchor begins after the hero.
- Purchase dock is compact and does not visually compete with the in-flow CTA.
- Full-width or asymmetric editorial sections are reserved for timeline, formula and ritual.

### 1440px+

- Maximum content width.
- Preserve composition tension without stretching line lengths.
- Use whitespace rather than enlarging every element.

## 8. Motion principles

Motion personality: **calm, weighty and responsive**.

Rules:

- Motion explains hierarchy, state or spatial relationship.
- Common actions receive shorter and quieter motion.
- Marketing entrances may be slightly longer, but never delay product comprehension.
- UI animation stays under 300ms.
- CSS transitions are preferred for predetermined interactions.
- Use springs only for interruptible swipe/drag gestures.
- Animate transform and opacity; avoid continuous layout/paint animation.
- Every interaction remains usable mid-transition.
- Keyboard-triggered actions receive immediate state changes, not decorative animation.

## 9. Motion register

| Element | Exact motion term | Proposed behavior | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero first load | Orchestration + stagger | Product image reveals first; decision panel fades/translates 8px; facts follow in a short cascade | 360–450ms total; 40–60ms stagger |
| Gallery next/previous | Direction-aware transition + crossfade | Frame moves 8–12px in navigation direction while old/new images crossfade | 220–260ms |
| Gallery swipe | Drag + momentum + rubber-banding | Direct manipulation with boundary resistance; threshold or velocity commits | Interruptible spring, low/no bounce |
| Gallery selector | Continuity transition | Active line/number moves between frames instead of blinking | 180–220ms |
| Anchor navigation | Continuity transition | Shared active indicator moves to the selected section | 180–220ms |
| Anchor jump | Scroll navigation | Measured-offset smooth scroll; active item auto-centers | 250–350ms; instant under reduced motion |
| Button press | Press/tap feedback | Scale to 0.97 with immediate release | 100–140ms |
| Add label | Crossfade + blur | Add to cart → Adding → Added/View cart without width jump | 140–180ms; success held about 2s |
| Quantity change | Continuity transition | Tabular digit crossfade/translate 4px; no rolling counter | 120–160ms |
| Purchase dock entry | Slide in + fade | Enters from its own height after hero action leaves view | 220–260ms enter; 160–190ms exit |
| Cart drawer | Slide in + fade | Drawer enters from bottom/mobile or side/desktop; backdrop fades | 240–280ms enter; 180–210ms exit |
| Accordion | Accordion/collapse | Panel expands without max-height clipping; chevron rotates with it | 220–260ms |
| Timeline/formula cards | Scroll reveal + stagger | Only these proof/education groups reveal once | 280–340ms; 40–60ms stagger |
| Error state | Feedback | Inline message appears with color/opacity; no shake unless input rejection is otherwise unclear | 140–180ms |
| Review scaffold | None | Static honest placeholder; no shimmer implying loading reviews | — |

Remove:

- Perpetual animation.
- Parallax.
- Marquees.
- Magnetic buttons.
- Decorative count-ups.
- Large blur transitions.
- Bounce/elastic easing.
- Motion on every lower-page section.

## 10. Motion tokens

Use a compact shared token set:

```css
:root {
  --ease-ui-out: cubic-bezier(.23, 1, .32, 1);
  --ease-ui-move: cubic-bezier(.77, 0, .175, 1);
  --ease-drawer: cubic-bezier(.32, .72, 0, 1);
  --dur-press: 120ms;
  --dur-fast: 160ms;
  --dur-ui: 220ms;
  --dur-panel: 260ms;
  --dur-reveal: 320ms;
}
```

Never use `transition: all`.

## 11. Reduced-motion behavior

Under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`:

- Remove translate, scale, parallax, stagger, blur and smooth scrolling.
- Keep color and opacity cues at 120–180ms where they clarify state.
- Gallery swaps immediately.
- Drawer and accordion open without positional animation.
- Add/loading/success labels still change and remain announced.
- Swipe remains functional but commits without animated travel.

## 13. Implementation phases and approval gates

### Phase 0 — Truth and content gate

- Remove unsupported review/count/verified-purchase content.
- Approve efficacy, safety, comparison and treatment language.
- Lock canonical product facts.

**Approval:** no UI work proceeds with knowingly false proof.

### Phase 1 — Static visual direction

- Build mobile 390 and desktop 1440 hero.
- Establish type, spacing, surfaces and recovery-line device.
- Present still screenshots without motion.

**Approval:** hierarchy, brand and first-two-viewports scan.

### Phase 2 — Responsive foundation

- Implement 320, 390, 768, 1024 and 1440 layouts.
- Build gallery, decision panel, purchase dock, anchor nav and drawer.

**Approval:** no overflow, overlap or late tablet decision failure.

### Phase 3 — Persuasion and image sequence

- Port timeline, formula, comparison, ritual, fit/deselect module, FAQ and heritage.
- Apply the six-image decision system and remove redundancy.

**Approval:** every section and image answers a unique shopper question.

### Phase 4 — State completeness

- Ready, hover, focus, pressed, adding, success, error, retry, disabled and out-of-stock.
- Keyboard, live-region, focus-trap and focus-return behavior.

**Approval:** purchase path works without motion or pointer input.

### Phase 5 — Motion implementation

- Implement only the approved register.
- Inspect in slow motion and frame by frame.
- Test interruption and rapid repeated actions.

**Approval:** motion improves orientation/feedback and never delays action.

### Phase 6 — Final audits

- Re-run `critique`, `audit`, responsive checks and image ledger.
- Test 200% zoom, reduced motion, keyboard and physical touch.
- Measure final Lighthouse/WebPageTest/CWV on the hosted implementation.
- Run `polish`.

**Approval:** 85+/100 minimum release gate; 90–93/100 design target; no P0/P1; no dimension below 4.0/5 for the design target.

## 15. Seed reference layer

The detailed pattern audit is in `SEED_REFERENCE_EVALUATION_AND_SCORE_LIFT.md`.

Use Seed's decision choreography, not its premium aesthetic:

1. Product recognition and one dominant action.
2. Progressive disclosure for fit, ingredients and cautions.
3. One-job sections with strong visual rhythm.
4. An honest expectation timeline.
5. A simple two-path mechanism visual.
6. A neutral, deselection-friendly routine comparison.
7. Verification separated from marketing claims.
8. Honest review distribution when real review data exists.
9. A sticky desktop decision panel and contextual mobile purchase dock.
10. Continuity and feedback motion instead of perpetual spectacle.

Explicitly avoid:

- Subscription-first pricing.
- Guarantee or risk-free language.
- Autoplay premium product spectacle.
- Dense proprietary-science language.
- Cinematic full-bleed media in every section.
- Unsupported reviews, ratings, customer counts, testing or certification.
- Simultaneous floating navigation and purchase layers that reduce mobile reading space.

The Seed-informed additions do not change the core direction: B remains the responsive commerce base, A remains the persuasion/content source, and the final Neeli prototype remains a value-conscious editorial recovery path.
